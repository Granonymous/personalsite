Adventures & Fieldwork

The Camino de Santiago

1 / 11

Walking 700+ miles across Spain on the Camino de Santiago was a journey of intentional simplicity. The rhythm of daily steps, the camaraderie of fellow pilgrims, and the constant presence of history transformed how I move through the world.

Patagonia Expedition

1 / 10

Spending six months in Patagonia as part of the National Outdoor Leadership School was my most transformative adventure. From scaling ice faces to navigating river crossings, the constant learning happened not just outdoors but in how I relate to challenge itself.

Sea Kayaking Adventure

1 / 9

Kayaking through coastal channels and islands provided both physical challenge and profound stillness. Each morning began with mist over water, each evening ended with the rhythm of waves—reminding me that the best insights come when movement becomes meditative.

About

I'm currently a student at Yale University pursuing a BS in Environmental Studies with a focus on Energy & Climate, complemented by a certificate in Statistics & Data Science. My work bridges the gap between environmental research and data-driven storytelling.

When I'm not working on geospatial analyses or writing about sustainability, you'll find me planning outdoor adventures—whether it's backpacking, kayaking, or preparing for my next expedition. My experiences range from co-founding a mushroom business to participating in the National Outdoor Leadership School's wilderness programs.

What connects all these threads is a commitment to understanding complex systems—whether ecological, social, or technological—and finding ways to communicate their stories effectively.
